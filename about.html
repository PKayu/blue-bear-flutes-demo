38 Years of Craftsmanship

The Story of
Blue Bear Flutes

From a personal love of music to a worldwide community of players — one maker, one instrument, decades of dedication.

The Maker

Charlie Mato-Toyela

My name — Mato-Toyela — translates to "Blue Bear" in the Lakota tradition. It's the name I've carried my whole life, and the name I've built this work around for 38 years. The bear is a symbol of strength, healing, and introspection — and to me, that's what this music is about.

I started making flutes because I fell in love with the sound. There's nothing quite like the voice of a Native American style flute — intimate, meditative, expressive in ways that even trained instrumentalists find remarkable. I wanted to share that.

What started as personal craft became a lifelong vocation. Along the way, I've refined the designs, explored dozens of wood species, developed the drone flute lineup, and built the Modern Series to bring these instruments to players who need consistency and accessibility without compromise.

Through it all, the goal has never changed: build flutes that play beautifully and connect people to music that matters.

1988

Blue Bear Flutes Founded

Charlie begins handcrafting Native American style flutes and sharing them with players across the United States.

2000s

Online Store & Community

Blue Bear Flutes moves online, reaching players in Europe, Asia, and South America for the first time. The Etsy shop opens and quickly earns a 4.9-star reputation.

2010s

YouTube & Free Lessons

Charlie begins teaching free lessons on YouTube, building a worldwide community of players who learn directly from the maker.

2020s

Modern Series Launch

The Blue Bear Modern Series brings Charlie's acoustic designs to precision 3D-printed instruments, opening the door to a new generation of players.

Today

4,400+ Flutes Shipped Worldwide

Over 1,000 five-star reviews. Players in dozens of countries. A living community of musicians who call Blue Bear Flutes their musical home.

Each flute is more than an instrument. It is the result of decades of hands-on craftsmanship, a deep love for music, and respect for traditions that have carried meaning across generations.

What We Stand For

The Blue Bear Promise

🌳

Real Materials

Eastern red cedar, western cedar, cherry, purpleheart, padauk, yellowheart — hand-selected wood from sustainable sources. Every species chosen for its unique acoustic character.

Charlie makes these flutes personally. Not a factory. Not a team of contractors. The hands that shaped the design shape every instrument that ships under the Blue Bear name.

Our Philosophy

Music for Everyone

The Native American style flute is one of the most accessible instruments in the world — it's pentatonic by nature, which means almost any note you play sounds musical. Charlie has spent 38 years building on that accessibility.

While many makers sell instruments and nothing else, Blue Bear Flutes includes you in a living community. Free lessons on YouTube and Instagram. Personal responses to questions. A social media family across Facebook, TikTok, Pinterest, and X.

You don't just buy a flute. You join the conversation.

And if you're interested in the craft itself, Charlie offers flute-making kits, the BBF Tone Box, and his book The Art of Native American Flute Making — everything you need to go from player to maker.
